Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a kind of cybercrime in which perpetrators pose as reputable companies to deceive people into divulging private information like passwords, bank account information, or OTPs. Emails, messages, or phoney websites that seem authentic are frequently the means by which it happens.

There is a distinct pattern to a typical phishing attack. The attacker starts by crafting a phoney message that appears to be from a reliable source, like a bank or a well-known internet service. The message then exhorts the victim to do something right away, like clicking a link or confirming account information.
The victim is taken to a phoney website that is made to appear authentic when they click the link. The attacker then records the victim's financial or personal information. Lastly, the attacker accesses accounts or perpetrates financial fraud using this information. Phishing attacks typically target older people, working professionals, and college students. 

Students are often targeted through fake internship offers or scholarship emails, while professionals may receive emails appearing to be from their workplace or banks.Phishing can have serious repercussions. Victims may experience financial loss from their bank accounts, identity theft, or illegal access to their email and social media accounts. A person's reputation and financial security may be impacted in certain situations.

Phishing is still one of the most prevalent and dangerous cyberthreats, so prevention requires awareness and caution.
